\hypertarget{introduction}{%
\section{Introduction}\label{introduction}}

Not just about covid. How to handle forecasting when there has been a
period of major structural change.

Not interested in identifying structural breaks -- we know they have
occured. How to forecast in the presence of such change.

Covid has provided one example that is affecting many forecasters around
the world. But similar ideas needed in many other contexts.

Time series models usually assume that the future is similar to the
past, at least in how the data evolve. But a big event like covid makes
the future different from the past, at least in the short term.

\hypertarget{handling-covid-effects-when-forecasting}{%
\section{Handling covid effects when
forecasting}\label{handling-covid-effects-when-forecasting}}

Some options are:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  Use a highly adaptive model -- e.g., ETS with large smoothing
  parameters
\end{enumerate}

Advantages: simple, easy to implement, fast to compute.

Disadvantages: large prediction intervals, because the model is
downweighting the past data. Fails to remember things before the change.
Also, the model may not adapt quickly enough to the change.

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\tightlist
\item
  Use a dynamic regression model with intervention covariates
\end{enumerate}

Advantages: retains full memory of the past, and allows the change
period to be effectively modelled provided you choose the covariates
well.

Disadvantages: requires a lot of thought to choose the covariates well.
Assumes that the post-pandemic period will be similar to the
pre-pandemic period.

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\setcounter{enumi}{2}
\tightlist
\item
  Treat the covid period as missing and use a model that handles missing
  values
\end{enumerate}

Advantages: effectively ignores the change period

Disadvantages: throws away the covid-period data, and so starts the
post-covid period with no recent history. Therefore prediction intervals
will be relatively large.

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\setcounter{enumi}{3}
\tightlist
\item
  Estimate what might have been and adjust the data
\end{enumerate}

Advantages: relatively simple and doesn't throw away the covid-period
data.

Disadvantages: need to have good estimates of covid-period data, and
that may be difficult to obtain. Forecasts are conditional on the
estimates being correct.
